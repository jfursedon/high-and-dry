High & Dry

Privacy Policy

Effective date: 11 July 2026

High & Dry is a free app that shows wet/dry climbing conditions for Southern Sandstone crags. In short: the app does not collect, store, or share any personal information about you. There are no accounts, no analytics, no advertising, and no tracking.

Information the app handles

High & Dry has no backend of its own and asks you for nothing. To show conditions it fetches weather forecasts from Open-Meteo using a fixed set of crag coordinates that are built into the app. Your own location is never requested, used, or transmitted.

Information sent to third parties

  • Open-Meteo — the app requests weather data for the built-in crag coordinates. As with any internet request, Open-Meteo receives standard network information such as your device's IP address. The app sends no personal data or identifiers. See the Open-Meteo privacy terms.
  • Windy.com — when you tap a crag to open the rain-radar map, the app loads Windy's map in an in-app web view. Windy then loads its own content and may receive your IP address and set its own cookies, governed by the Windy privacy policy.

The app does not send your data to any other service, and does not send anything to the developer.

Information stored on your device

The app stores a small amount of data locally on your device only:

  • the most recent wet/dry verdict for each crag, so it can tell when a crag has newly dried out;
  • your notification preference.

This data never leaves your device and is removed when you delete the app.

Notifications

If you allow notifications, the app may send you a local notification when a crag dries out to "green". These are generated on your device. There is no push server and no message is sent to or from the developer.

Tracking, analytics, and advertising

None. The app contains no analytics SDKs, no advertising, and no third-party tracking, and it does not build a profile of you.

Children's privacy

The app collects no personal information from anyone, including children.
